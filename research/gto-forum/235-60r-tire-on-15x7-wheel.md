### Replies

**@Sick467** (reply #1):
According to the "Tire and Rim Association", putting a 235/60R15 on a 7" rim is entirely acceptable and ideal, but notice that the Approved Rim Contours for this tire size goes from 6-1/2 to 8-1/2...that 235/60R15 tire should be able to be mounted to any rim in this range, but the ideal rim width is 7" to get the most out of the tire.

    
        
            
        
        
            
                
                
            
        
    
    

Not to say an 8" rim would not work, but not idea by the standard.  As far as what fits a '64...I'll leave that to the '64 owners and their experiences.  I have put smaller tires on wider than recommended rims and they don't look right...way too rounded where the sidewalls meet the tread.  These were for rollers during the rebuild process and not for street use, mind you.  If you are going for looks, adjust backspacing to get the tire out were you want it with the 7" rim and the 235/60R15, if you are going for tread on the pavement, look into going with a wider tire with less side wall on the 8" rim.  The 235\60R15 tire will like being on the 7" rim more compared to the 8".  You gain nothing by going to the 8" rim with this size tire.  You will probably lose a bit of traction actually.  If you need more of a beefy look or traction back there with a wider rim...I suggest you go with a different tire size on the 8" rim.  If you look at the same size tire on two different size rims that cross that threshold, you will find that the tread to pavement width can lessen with the wider rim due to the stretching of the side wall to make up for the added width.  It pulls the tread towards the side wall, or rim, and off the pavement.

**@Sick467** (reply #4):
Something to keep in mind...when I was doing research for my rear wheels and tires, I found that the same size tire fit better in the wheel well on an 8" rim when compared to the larger 9" rim.  The wider rim could cause the increased tire sectional width to rub whereas the 8" rim tucked it in much better.  The resulting tread on the pavement was practically the same, if not a bit better.  I doubt this will be an issue with your selected size tire, depending on the back spacing you choose.
