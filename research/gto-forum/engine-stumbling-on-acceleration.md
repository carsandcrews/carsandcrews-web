**@geeteeohguy** (reply #35):
Your problem with driving down the road and needing to baby it is way past it being a pump shot issue. You likely have fuel starvation issues due to a clogged sock in the fuel tank, the rubber fuel line at the tank to the steel line being porous, or junk from the tank in the new carb clogging up the jets. First step is to disconnect the fuel line at the carb and do a cranking fuel volume test. Pressure is one thing, but you need volume also. It could also be a low float level in the carb. A low float adjustment will cause all of your issues. A weak pump shot will only cause a stumble right out of a stop light. After the car is rolling, it will run just fine on the other carb circuits.
