**@Sick467** (reply #3):
Bench work may be required.  I have hammer-tapped studs (with some gusto) on stuck bolts/studs on the bench/vise with success, but I would not try hitting them that hard while bolted to the engine.  The impact of a hammer directly in line with the stud/bolt can jar the rust loose and free them up.  I will use a long brass nut (barrel nut) or two nuts doubled up on the end of the stud to take the hammer blow. 

Fast heat at the manifold may work for you.  You need to heat the manifold area around the stud fast so that the heat does not absorb into the stud and cause it to swell with the manifold. This causes the manifold steel to swell before the stud has a chance to swell along with it.   If the heat application is slow, they both expand at the same rate and stay bound.   It is equally as important to apply the loosening torque to the stud before the stud swells from the heat.  An induction heater may not be fast enough to swell the manifold before the stud grows as well.  A map torch or small acetylene torch has the best chance of working on the manifold and you don't want the steel to glow orange or too much heat has soaked into the stud already.

**@Lok** (reply #7):
Since it's still on the car work will be hard to get a good angle. Here is an option, put a stack of standard flat washers and then nut it up tight with a nut then stack nuts on it till the threads are completely covered, heat it up to standard red hot, not so hot it mealts the steel. Then with it hot shock the nut covered stud with a few moderate hammer hits strait on. **@Spode Oden** (reply #21):
Here’s a trick I learned from an old guy who restored old tractors. It makes sense and it works with patience. You need to heat the bolt only. The bolt tries to expand inside the cold casting. As it cools, it shrinks, loosening the fit and busting the rust. Do this for several cycles, letting the assembly completely cool. Each time the bolt shrinks more. Eventually it will turn out. If the bolt is broken off, place a larger nut and try to weld the inside of the nut to the stud. The welder heats the bolt only. The nut will twist off and you try it again and again. When the stud has shrunk enough, it will twist out. it takes time to let the assembly cool every time. 
I’ve done it on my own machinery that looked hopeless and it works!!
