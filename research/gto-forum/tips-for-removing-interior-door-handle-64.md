## The Issue
Hey there. I'm not having any luck getting the door handle off. Probably hasn't been removed in 30yrs. I've tried all angles with different tools without success. ## Solution / Outcome
One thing I didn't notice/realize until today is that the tool needs to slide into the grooves to go far enough to push the pins. Seems obvious now, but those frontal points get bent easily.  Someone mentioned bending the tool but I was wasn't thinking specifically about the pointa.

**@Sick467** (reply #14):
You've got yourself in a pickle, but you can get out of it.   I have been in the exact same situation.  This is what the clip is supposed to look like (you probably know this)...

    
        
            
        
        
            
                
                
            
        
    
    

Here is a pic of the back of the handle with the clip installed...

    
        
            
        
        
            
                
                
            
        
    
    

You can see the tips of the clip at the big red arrows and the rounded part inside the hole has the little arrow.

    
        
            
        
        
            
                
                
            
        
    
    

Those rounded parts snap into the grove in the door handle mechanism at the red line below.

    
        
            
        
        
            
                
                
            
        
    
    

You have probably popped a clip tip into the grove past the handle casing and it's giving you fits.  Straighten out the curvy piece you have showing but leave a bit of twist to it so you can get a hold of it with pliers (it may come out just straightening it out).  If not, this is where the wiggling comes in again...wiggle the clip wire (with some force, but a little at a time) back and forth, in and out, in circles, any pattern really...at the same time you are pushing/pulling the handle in and out (it may not move much at all, ut in an out may give you a feel if the clip's resistance changes).   If it does not cooperate, increase the force and decrease the frequency of the wiggles.  This may help straighten out the clip that is bent up inside the handle hole and allow it to fish out.

Think of it as you are trying to pick a lock...there is a combination of force, position, tension, & interference that will "un-lock" it.

Just don't poke a hole in the door panel.  Some dense hard thin carboard (think soda or cereal carton) taped to the panel around the handle might make working on it less risky.  Don't ask how I know...LOL!  God Speed Kevin.
